Lightning is a natural phenomenon consisting of electrostatic discharges occurring through the atmosphere between two electrically charged regions. One or both regions are within the atmosphere, with the second region sometimes occurring on the ground. Following the lightning, the regions become partially or wholly electrically neutralized.

üß¨ Structure of Atoms

The structure of an atom consists of a central nucleus surrounded by electrons. The nucleus contains protons (positively charged) and neutrons (neutral), while electrons (negatively charged) orbit around the nucleus. Most of the atom is empty space, with the nucleus being much smaller than the entire atom. The arrangement of these particles determines the chemical behavior of the atom and its interactions with other atoms.

üéà Static Electricity

Static electricity occurs when there is an imbalance of electric charges on an object. This imbalance typically arises from friction, which transfers electrons from one material to another. For example, when you rub a balloon on your hair, electrons move from your hair to the balloon, causing the balloon to become negatively charged and your hair to become positively charged.
